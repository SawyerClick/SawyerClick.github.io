Subway Ridership

New Yorkers are raging against the (subway) machines

NYC's Summer of Hell has come and gone, but the subway system's ridership is still in a state of decline.

By Sawyer Click

Published July 29, 2019

A NEW LOW

The ridership plummet has brought the overall ridership to its lowest point in years. The Summer of Hell has come and gone, but it doesn't seem as it service is getting better.

Gov. Cuomo's charge to fix the MTA has been brewing for a year, with the most recent stab at the growing homeless population crowding stations and trains. Recently, the MTA announced that on-time service was above 80%, marking a milestone. Still, ridership is falling.

Each borough suffered a loss for the second year straight. Brooklyn and Manhattan have had a ridership dip for three years. Each, except for Queens, had a larger drop than the year before.

Borough by Borough

Stations that lost riders dwarf those that gained. Manhattan was closest to breaking even with stations that lost and stations that gained riders.
