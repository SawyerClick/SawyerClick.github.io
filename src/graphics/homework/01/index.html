Subways

The subway system is really struggling

With Manhattan suffering the most, each borough averaged a loss of subway rides. Some stations fell harder than others.

By Sawyer Click

Published September 9, 2019

After scraping the MTA's subway ridership data for a project detailing the system's extreme ridership loss, I charted out each station's ridership change from 2017 to 2018.

Using ai2html and Adobe Illustrator, I tweaked this image to responsively act to browser and device size. The largest two models are relatively the same, just a different size. The smallest model breaks the boroughs apart and groups them in pairs rather than presenting them all side by side.

Manhattan fell the hardest

A majority of stations in each borough lost riders by the millions from 2017 to 2018, with Manhattan facing the most severe losses.

Manhattan suffered far more than the other boroughs, with two stations losing more than 3 million rides. No other borough crossed the -2 million threshold. The large number of stations that lost ridership can be attributed to maintenance efforts.

Across the board, the pairing of Brooklyn and Manhattan makes more sense as the two had the startest contrasts. The Bronx and Queens were both lukewarm, though the boroughs still lost more rides than they gained.
